Limitations of HLS on Android

The Android MediaPlayer has problems with playing HLS videos. The Brightcove Player SDK for Android solves these issues, allowing you to play HLS videos without these limitations.

Learn more about Brightcove's solutions for playing HLS video on Android devices:

  1. Using the Brightcove Player SDK for Android to play HLS videos
  2. Using the Brightcove HLS Player to play HLS videos

Known limitations

If you choose to use the Android MediaPlayer without one of Brightcove's solutions listed above, you may experience the following issues with HLS playback:

  • Android 2.3
    • HLS and Widevine playback not supported by SDK due to OS limitations.

      The Brightcove HLS Player adds support for HLS playback.

  • Android 3.x
    • Not supported
  • Android 4.x
    • HLS streams only seek to the beginning of segments due to an OS bug; the Brightcove HLS Player does not have this limitation.

      As a result, midroll ads are only supported with non-HLS streams, with the Brightcove Player SDK (Android 4.1 or newer), or with the Brightcove HLS Player.

    • HLS streams do not consistently report the correct video duration due to an OS bug.

      The Brightcove Player SDK (Android 4.1 or newer) and the Brightcove HLS Player do not have this limitation.

    • The timer, counter, Learn More button overlay and some AdEvent events are missing with Google IMA midrolls due to a reported bug in the Google IMA SDK.

    • We have seen issues with Widevine content on some Samsung devices. The Widevine teams advises upgrading to the latest Android version.
  • Android 4.4.2
    • Playback will start, pause after a few seconds and then play from the start again.

FAQ

How can I get HLS to work on Android?

To be able to provide adaptive streaming on Android, Brightcove uses a HLS Player add-on to backfill HLS functionality to Android 2.3 and provide a better HLS playback functionality with Android 4.x. Contact your account manager for more information regarding the options and pricing for the HLS Player.

Brightcove's Player SDK provides better HLS playback functionality for Android 4.1 and newer.

What about HLSe?

HLSe has the same limitations and recommendations as above.
