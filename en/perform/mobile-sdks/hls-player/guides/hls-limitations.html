Limitations of HLS on Android

The HLS Player is an add-on to the Player SDK for Android. Because it leverages a VisualOn player, it does require additional native libraries to be included in your app and has additional costs. Please contact your Brightcove account manager for pricing details.

This topic outlines the limitations that Brightcove has witnessed on using HLS on Android. Since the Brightcove Player SDK for Android leverages underlying Media Player components from Android, the SDK inherits similar behavior.

Known limitations

  • Android 2.3
    • HLS and Widevine playback not supported by SDK due to OS limitations; HLS Player adds support for HLS playback.
  • Android 3.x
    • Not supported
  • Android 4.x
    • HLS streams only seek to the beginning of segments due to an OS bug; the HLS Player does not have this limitation. As a result, midroll ads are only supported with non-HLS streams or with the HLS Player.
    • HLS streams do not consistently report the correct video duration due to an OS bug; the HLS Player does not have this limitation.
    • The timer, counter, Learn More button overlay and some AdEvent events are missing with Google IMA midrolls due to a reported bug in the Google IMA SDK.
    • We have seen issues with Widevine content on some Samsung devices. The Widevine teams advises upgrading to the latest Android version.
  • Android 4.4.2
    • Playback will start, pause after a few seconds and then play from the start again.

FAQ

How can I get HLS to work on Android?

To be able to provide adaptive streaming on Android, Brightcove uses an HLS Player add-on to backfill HLS functionality to Android 2.3 and provide a better HLS playback functionality with Android 4.x. Contact your account manager for more information regarding the options and pricing for the HLS Player.

Are there any other options for adaptive streaming?

Widevine playback on Android provides its own proprietary adaptive streaming technology but is coupled with Widevine encryption and DRM.

What about HLSe?

HLSe has the same limitations and recommendations as above.
