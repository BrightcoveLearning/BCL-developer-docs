Learning Guide - Brightcove Beacon

Introduction

Brightcove Beacon is an SaaS-based OTT platform that permits you to deliver OTT experiences in record time.

To be sure you have a good basis of knowledge on which to build, it is best to be sure the term OTT is understood.

What is OTT?

According to Wikipedia, over-the-top (OTT) media services are streaming media services offered directly to viewers over the Internet. OTT bypasses traditional cable, broadcast and satellite television platforms that traditionally act as a controller or distributor of such content. Some existing OTT services include Prime Video, Hulu and Netflix.

More simply, OTT is for companies who own or buy media of some type and wish to sell it for people to watch.

Not only can OTT content can be viewed from traditional desktop, laptop and tablet computers, but also needs to provide viewing from mobile phones, smart TVs (Samsung, LG, etc.), set-top “boxes” (Apple TV, Fire TV, Roku, etc.) and gaming consoles (Playstation, Xbox, etc.).

VOD versus OTT

To be sure the difference between these two, sometimes confusing and often incorrectly considered to be the same thing, terms, let’s clarify. VOD (video on demand) generally refers to video on demand services through cable networks. OTT on the other hand is transmitted to the viewer via the Internet without requiring users to subscribe to a traditional cable service.

Now that OTT is defined, it will be easier to understand what Brightcove Beacon provides in the space.

What is Brightcove Beacon?

Brightcove Beacon is a platform that enables you to create and configure an OTT experience, which is then used to generate multiple device apps. The device apps generated are grouped into the following five categories (some specific examples included):

  • Mobile (Phone / Tablets)
    • iOS
    • Android
  • Connected TVs
    • Roku
    • Apple TV
    • Fire TV
    • Android TV
  • Smart TVs
    • LG
    • Samsung
    • Sony
    • Toshiba
  • Consoles
    • Playstation
    • XBOX
  • Web
