Ad types

There are three main types of video advertising, they are:

  • Linear ads: Linear ads occur during playback of the video and are displayed in the player itself. The ad interrupts video playback either before (pre-roll), during (mid-roll) or after (post-roll) consumption of the video content by the user.
  • Non-linear ads: Non-linear ads run during playback so the users see the ad while viewing the content. Non-linear ads can be text, graphical ads or even video overlays. An example non-linear ad is a text overlay that displays over a playing video, but does not interrupt the video. The following example shows an overlay the user can click to go to another page. The overlay appears for some specified time, but does not interrupt playback of the video.
  • Companion ads: Companion ads are text or display ads that appear around the video player, generally tied to the video content. An example companion ad is a banner that is tuned to video content. Ad standards

The Interactive Advertising Bureau (IAB) is advertising business organization of more than 650 leading media and technology companies that that develops industry standards, conducts research, and provides legal support for the online advertising industry.

Google is also a major player in video advertising has defined the Google Interactive Media Ads (IMA) SDKs that enable publishers to display linear, non-linear, and companion ads in videos and games. The IMA3 plugin for Brightcove Player integrates the Brightcove player with Google's Interactive Media Ads (IMA) for HTML5 version 3. This allows you to request and track VAST ads for your player.

The following are key standards for video advertising:

  • VMAP - From IAB's standards: "The IAB Video Multiple Ad Playlist (VMAP) specification is an XML template that video content owners can use to describe the structure for ad inventory insertion when they don’t control the video player or the content distribution outlet."
    • In other words, VMAP is the playlist of ads and when they should be shown.
    • Google term for VMAP is "ad rules".
  • VAST - From IAB's standards: "The Video Ad Serving Template (VAST) includes a standard XML-based ad response for in-stream video as well as an XML Schema Definition (“XSD”) for developers. It is meant to accommodate the majority of current practices within the online digital video advertising business."
    • In other words, VMAP may say there is a pre-roll, VAST describes that pre-roll.
    • Google term for VAST document is "ad tag".
  • VPAID - From IAB's standards" "The Video Player Ad-Serving Interface Definition (VPAID) establishes a common interface between video players and ad units, enabling a rich interactive in-stream ad experience."
    • In other words, VPAID is like VAST for interactive ads.
    • At this time the IMA3 plugin for Brightcove Player does not support VPAID.

Flash vs. HTML

At this time the preponderance of video ads for desktop are in Flash. For this reason the IMA3 plugin for Brightcove Player's adTechOrder option is set to flash first then html5.

The strong influence of iOS in the mobile scene, and the lack of Flash in iOS, means that the adTechOrder option for Brightcove Native Player SDKs is set to html5 first then flash.

Parties in video advertising

Although many groupings of the parties involved in video advertising exist, a very simple example follows:

  • Publishers - Have video content to show, and want to use ads to gain revenue.
  • Advertisers - Have ads they want customers/potential customers to view.
  • Ad networks - Aggregator of ad space supplied by publishers, then match the ad space to advertisers.
  • Video Technology Providers - Provide encoding and player technologies to publishers and ad networks. Brightcove fits into the video technology providers.
