Managing Video Bandwidth in Video Cloud

This topic explains how bandwidth is used - and wasted - by online video, and things you can do to minimize wasted bandwidth.

Video bandwidth

Like any other digital asset, a video is delivered over the internet to browsers and apps as data. The amount of data transferred is the bandwidth consumed, and you are charged for. This bandwidth can be divided into two kinds:

  • Used bandwidth: the data associated with part(s) of the video that the viewer watches
  • Wasted bandwidth: data associated with part(s) of the video that are downloaded, but not actually viewed (we'll look below at why and how this happens)

We can further identify, in some cases, a portion of the used bandwidth that we'll call excessive bandwidth. Excessive bandwidth is consumed when a high quality rendition of the video is viewed, but is indistinguishable to the viewer from a lower quality rendition. This occurs when the size and/or visual resolution of the video rendition is greater than the viewer's screen supports.
